Nelson Mandela
Anti‑apartheid leader • President of South Africa (1994–1999)
  • Born: 18 July 1918 — Mvezo, South Africa
  • Died: 5 December 2013 — Johannesburg
  • Also known as: Madiba
  • Nobel Peace Prize: 1993
Key Contributions
  • Led resistance to apartheid; championed reconciliation and human rights.
  • Helped negotiate the end of apartheid and South Africa’s first multiracial elections.
  • As president, focused on nation‑building, equality, and democratic institutions.
Milestones (Ordered)
  1. 1944 — Co‑founded the ANC Youth League.
  2. 1962 — Arrested; later sentenced to life imprisonment.
  3. 1990 — Released after 27 years in prison.
  4. 1993 — Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize.
  5. 1994 — Elected President of South Africa.
Selected Quotes
  • “It always seems impossible until it’s done.”
  • “Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world.”
